# Who We're Building For

## The Problem

Booking things shouldn't be hard. But it usually is.

You want to reserve a tennis court? Call the facility, hope someone answers, figure out what's available, write it down somewhere, show up and hope they actually have your booking. Want a haircut? Same story. Playground for a birthday party? Even worse.

Phone calls. WhatsApp messages. Scrap paper. Forgotten appointments.

We can do better.

## Our Users

### The Customer Side

**Alex — the regular athlete**

Alex plays volleyball twice a week after work. He knows exactly what he wants: see available courts, pick a time, book it, show up. He doesn't want to call anyone. He doesn't want to wonder if the booking actually went through. He just wants to play.

*What Alex needs:* Mobile-friendly, quick booking, instant confirmation.

**Maria — the busy professional**

Maria's schedule is packed. She needs to book hair appointments, maybe a massage, maybe a consultation here and there. She often forgets until the last minute. She needs reminders.

*What Maria needs:* Calendar integration, push notifications, easy rescheduling.

**The Thompson family**

They're planning their kid's birthday party. Need a playground for Saturday afternoon. But which ones are available? What do they cost? Can they bring their own food?

*What they need:* Clear information upfront, photos, reviews from other families, simple payment.

### The Business Side

**Dmitry — sports facility manager**

Dmitry runs a sports complex with four tennis courts, two volleyball courts, and a football field. His biggest headaches: double bookings, no-shows, and the constant phone calls asking "do you have anything for tonight?"

*What Dmitry needs:* A dashboard showing all bookings at once. Automatic availability. Online payments so people actually show up.

**Elena — salon owner**

Elena has three hairdressing chairs and two nail stations. She used to manage everything in a paper notebook. It worked until it didn't. Now she's drowning in texts and missed appointments.

*What Elena needs:* Each chair as a separate bookable resource. Clients book themselves. She sees her day at a glance.

**The community center team**

They manage public spaces — playgrounds, meeting rooms, event halls. Different rules for different spaces. Some need deposits. Some have time limits. Some are free.

*What they need:* Flexible rules per resource. Reporting for the municipality. Fair allocation when demand exceeds supply.

### The Platform Side

**The system administrator**

Someone needs to keep the lights on. Handle user issues. Make sure payments process correctly. See when something's wrong before users complain.

## The Ecosystem Angle

Here's where things get more interesting.

Many of our service providers aren't just booking time slots. They're offering expertise. A personal trainer doesn't just book an hour — they bring knowledge. Exercise programs. Nutrition advice. Video tutorials.

That's where [LinkSaver](../../linksaver) comes in.

**Picture this:** A trainer organizes their materials in LinkSaver — workout videos, meal plans, technique breakdowns. When a client books a session through Ajasta, the trainer can attach relevant resources. The client gets their appointment *and* supporting materials.

It works for consultants too. Lawyers sharing document templates. Tutors sharing practice problems. Coaches sharing strategy guides.

Knowledge management meets scheduling. That's the ecosystem we're building.

## What Drives Adoption

For customers:
- Convenience (book in 30 seconds vs. 15 minutes on the phone)
- Reliability (instant confirmation, no "I thought you said 3pm")
- Transparency (see all options, compare prices, read reviews)

For businesses:
- Time savings (fewer phone calls, fewer scheduling conflicts)
- Reduced no-shows (prepayment, reminders)
- Professional image (modern booking system vs. "let me check my notebook")

## Geographic Focus

Starting with Russian-speaking markets. The need is universal, but local presence matters for trust and support.

## The Competition

Of course we're not alone. There's Calendly for meetings, Booksy for beauty services, various niche booking systems.

Our angle: flexibility. One platform that handles sports facilities, service appointments, and event spaces. Plus the LinkSaver integration for knowledge-sharing service providers.

We're not trying to be the best meeting scheduler. We're trying to be the best *resource* scheduler for physical spaces and in-person services.
